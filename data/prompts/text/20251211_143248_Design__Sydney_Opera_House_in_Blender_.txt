# Sydney Opera House - Professional 3D Modeling Specification

## I. OVERALL STRUCTURE AND DIMENSIONS

### Primary Measurements
- **Total Length:** 185 meters (607 feet) east-west axis
- **Maximum Width:** 120 meters (394 feet) north-south axis
- **Tallest Shell Height:** 67 meters (220 feet) above sea level
- **Podium Height:** 25 meters (82 feet) above sea level
- **Total Footprint:** 1.8 hectares (4.5 acres)
- **Site Elevation:** Built on Bennelong Point, projecting into Sydney Harbour

### Fundamental Geometric Principle
All roof shells derive from a single sphere with radius **75.2 meters**. This revolutionary design solution by Jørn Utzon allows all shells to be constructed using identical geometric principles, creating structural and aesthetic unity. Each shell represents a spherical section cut at different angles and positions.

### Three Primary Shell Assemblies

**1. Concert Hall Complex (Western Group)**
- Two major shells + one minor side shell
- Largest shell: 67m height, 45m base width, 88m curve length
- Second shell: 62m height, 42m base width
- Minor shell: 45m height, 28m base width
- Inter-shell gap: 3.2 meters
- Total rib count: 92-96 ribs across all shells

**2. Joan Sutherland Theatre (Eastern Group)**
- Two major shells + one minor side shell
- Largest shell: 58m height, 40m base width
- Second shell: 55m height, 38m base width
- Minor shell: 42m height, 25m base width
- Inter-shell gap: 2.8 meters
- Slightly smaller proportions than Concert Hall

**3. Bennelong Restaurant Complex (Western Peninsula)**
- Four smaller interconnected shells
- Heights: 28-35 meters
- More compact, intimate scale
- Integrated into podium western extension

### Spatial Relationships
- Shell base angle: 60-65° from horizontal at steepest sections
- Apex curvature: Approaches 85-90° (near-vertical)
- Platform extension: 30-40 meters beyond shell footprints
- Total shell surface area: 25,700 square meters (6.225 acres)

**Western Approach (Forecourt):**
- **Broadwalk width:** 100 meters
- **Material:** Swedish Sälen pink granite, thermally treated
- **Paving unit size:** 1.2m × 0.6m × 100mm thick
- **Pattern:** Running bond with 10mm joints
- **Expansion joints:** 20mm wide, every 6 meters

**Monumental Staircase:**
- **Total steps:** 97 steps (main western approach)
- **Tread depth:** 400mm
- **Riser height:** 150mm (very shallow for monumental effect)
- **Nosing:** 30mm overhang, 15mm radius
- **Width:** Varies 40-80 meters (widening toward base)
- **Landings:** Every 15-20 steps, 4m deep, 8m+ wide
- **Handrails:** Stainless steel, 50mm diameter tube, posts every 1.8m

**Colonnade System:**
- **Column spacing:** 6.0 meters on center
- **Column section:** 800mm × 800mm square
- **Column height:** 8.0 meters

- Vehicle ramps: Southern side, 1:10 slope
- Service tunnels: Connect all major spaces
- Mechanical rooms: Integrated within podium mass

### B. Shell Roof Structure System

**Rib Construction Method:**
Each shell consists of precast concrete ribs radiating from a common base point, creating a fan-like structure.

**Rib Specifications:**
- **Rib spacing:** 2.4 meters center-to-center (constant)
- **Rib profile:** T-section with varying depth
- **Base depth:** 1.8 meters
- **Apex depth:** 0.6 meters (tapers continuously)
- **Rib width:** 1.2 meters (constant)
- **Material:** Precast reinforced concrete, high-strength mix

**Precast Segment System:**
- **Segments per rib:** 60-70 segments (depending on rib length)
- **Segment length:** 1.2-1.5 meters
- **Segment weight:** 8-10 tons each
- **Total segments (entire building):** 2,194 unique pieces
- **Connection method:** Post-tensioned cables through rib cores
- **Cable diameter:** 32mm high-tensile steel
- **Tensioning force:** 800-1200 kN per cable

**Rib-to-Rib Connections:**
- **Lateral ties:** Steel cables every 4.8 meters vertically
- **Cable spacing:** Creates diamond-pattern bracing
- **Diaphragm arches:** Concrete arches at base and mid-height for stability
- **Joint tolerance:** ±5mm positional accuracy required

**Shell Quantities by Group:**

*Concert Hall:*
- Major Shell 1: 36 ribs, 2,340 segments
- Major Shell 2: 34 ribs, 2,210 segments
- Minor Shell 3: 24 ribs, 1,440 segments

*Opera Theatre:*
- Major Shell 4: 32 ribs, 2,080 segments
- Major Shell 5: 30 ribs, 1,950 segments
- Minor Shell 6: 22 ribs, 1,320 segments

*Bennelong:*
- Four shells: 18-24 ribs each, 1,080-1,440 segments each

## IV. WEATHERING AND AGING DETAILS

### A. Tile Surface Weathering

**Salt Spray Effects (Harbor Environment):**
- **Affected area:** Lower 10-15 meters of shells, harbor-facing sides
- **Appearance:** White crystalline deposits
- **Coverage:** 5-15% of affected area
- **Deposit thickness:** 0.1-0.5mm
- **Color:** Pure white (RGB 255, 255, 255)
- **Distribution:** Concentrated in horizontal surfaces and crevices

**Atmospheric Soiling:**
- **General dulling:** Glossy tiles reduced to 70-80% original gloss
- **Dirt accumulation:** Gray-brown deposits in sheltered areas
- **Thickness:** 0.05-0.2mm
- **Color:** Gray-brown (RGB 140, 135, 125)
- **Pattern:** More pronounced in rain-sheltered areas

**Biological Growth:**
- **Organism type:** Algae and lichen
- **Color:** Dark green to black (RGB 40, 50, 35)
- **Location:** Damp, shaded areas; tile grout lines
- **Coverage:** 2-5% of total surface in affected areas
- **Thickness:** 0.1-1mm
- **Pattern:** Irregular patches and streaks

**Tile Replacement and Repairs:**
- **Replacement rate:** Approximately 0.5% of tiles replaced over 50 years
- **New tile appearance:** Slightly brighter white, cleaner
- **Color difference:** ΔE = 3-5 (noticeable but not jarring)
- **Distribution:** Random, scattered throughout shells
- **Identification:** Cleaner grout lines around new tiles

**Glaze Degradation:**
- **Microcracking:** Fine crazing on 1-2% of glossy tiles
- **Crack width:** <0.1mm (not visible beyond 2 meters)
- **Pattern:** Random network
- **Effect:** Slight dulling of gloss in affected tiles

### B. Concrete Weathering

**Water Staining:**
- **Location:** Below all joints, drainage points, and shell valleys
- **Pattern:** Vertical streaks, 50-200mm wide
- **Color:** Darker gray-green (RGB 120, 125, 115)
- **Length:** 0.5-3 meters below source
- **Intensity:** Darker near source, fading downward
- **Cause:** Mineral leaching and algae growth

**Efflorescence:**
- **Appearance:** White powdery or crystalline deposits
- **Composition:** Calcium carbonate and calcium sulfate
- **Location:** 10-15% of concrete surfaces, random distribution
- **Thickness:** 0.1-0.5mm
- **Color:** White to light gray (RGB 240, 240, 235)
- **Pattern:** Irregular patches, often near cracks

**Surface Erosion:**
- **Depth:** 0.5-2mm over 50 years
- **Pattern:** Slight aggregate exposure on weather-exposed faces
- **Effect:** Rougher texture, increased surface area
- **Location:** Primarily southern and western exposures (prevailing weather)

**Spalling (Concrete Damage):**
- **Frequency:** 0.5% of rib surface area
- **Patch size:** 20-100mm diameter
- **Depth:** 5-20mm
- **Cause:** Rebar corrosion, freeze-thaw, impact
- **Appearance:** Irregular craters exposing aggregate and rebar
- **Repair evidence:** Some patches filled with matching concrete (lighter color)

**Cracking:**
- **Crack type:** Hairline shrinkage cracks
- **Crack width:** 0.5-2mm
- **Pattern:** Random, some following formwork lines
- **Frequency:** 1-2 cracks per rib segment
- **Color:** Darker than surrounding concrete (dirt accumulation)
- **Depth:** Surface cracks, not structural

### C. Glass Weathering and Soiling

### C. Shadow and Ambient Occlusion

**Shell-to-Shell Shadows:**
- **Shadow type:** Hard shadows in direct sun, soft in overcast
- **Shadow color:** Cool blue-gray (RGB 180, 190, 200) - influenced by sky
- **Shadow intensity:** 60-80% darkening in direct sun
- **Shadow complexity:** Multiple overlapping shadows from shell array
- **Self-shadowing:** Ribs cast shadows on tiles (0.3-1.2m width depending on sun angle)

**Rib Shadow Characteristics:**
- **Shadow pattern:** Regular, rhythmic pattern every 2.4 meters
- **Shadow sharpness:** Sharp in direct sun, diffuse in overcast
- **Shadow color:** Cooler than surrounding tiles (blue-shifted)
- **Shadow width variation:** Narrow at apex (0.3m), wider at base (1.2m)
- **Shadow intensity:** 40-60% darkening

**Ambient Occlusion Settings:**
- **AO Distance:** 2-3 meters (architectural scale)
- **AO Samples:** 16-32 (high quality)
- **AO Color:** Neutral gray (RGB 128, 128, 128) or slight blue tint (RGB 120, 125, 130)

**High AO Areas (Darkest):**
- Inter-shell gaps (3-meter gaps): 60-80% darkening
- Under shell overhangs: 50-70% darkening
- Rib-to-tile junctions: 30-50% darkening
- Mullion corners and reveals: 40-60% darkening
- Podium colonnade corners: 50-70% darkening
- Grout lines: 20-30% darkening (subtle)

**AO Falloff:**
- **Falloff curve:** Quadratic (rapid falloff near contact, gradual at distance)
- **Maximum influence:** 3 meters
- **Minimum influence:** 0 at 3+ meters

### D. Reflection and Refraction

**Tile Reflections:**
- **Glossy tiles:** Sharp reflections of sky, sun, surrounding buildings
- **Reflection intensity:** 8-12% at perpendicular view, 40-60% at grazing angles
- **Reflection color:** Tinted by tile color (warm white)
- **Reflection clarity:** Clear, minimal distortion
- **Matte tiles:** Diffuse reflections only, no clear images

**Glass Reflections:**
- **Exterior reflections:** Sky, harbor, surrounding buildings
- **Reflection intensity:** 8% perpendicular, 95%+ at grazing angles (Fresnel)
- **Reflection color:** Tinted bronze
- **Reflection clarity:** Sharp, mirror-like
- **Interior reflections:** Interior lights, people, furnishings (at night)

**Glass Refraction:**
- **Refraction strength:** IOR 1.52
- **Visible effect:** Slight distortion of objects behind glass
- **Color shift:** Bronze tint applied to transmitted light
- **Caustics:** Subtle caustic patterns on interior floors from sunlight through glass
- **Dispersion:** Minimal chromatic aberration (0.01)

**Water Reflections (Harbor):**
- **Reflection clarity:** Broken, rippled (water surface normal variation)
- **Reflection intensity:** 20-40% depending on angle and water roughness
- **Color shift:** Blue tint from water absorption
- **Caustics:** Dynamic caustic patterns on underwater surfaces and nearby structures

### E. Atmospheric Effects

**Atmospheric Haze:**
- **Distance:** Visible beyond 500 meters
- **Color:** Light blue-gray (RGB 200, 210, 220)
- **Density:** Low (Sydney's clear air)
- **Effect:** Slight desaturation and lightening of distant objects
- **Scattering:** Rayleigh scattering (blue sky)

**Fog (Occasional):**
- **Frequency:** Rare, early morning
- **Density:** Light to medium
- **Color:** Neutral gray-white (RGB 220, 220, 220)
- **Visibility:** 100-500 meters
- **Effect:** Softens shadows, reduces contrast

**Salt Spray (Near Harbor):**
- **Visibility:** Slight haze near water surface
- **Effect:** Slight reduction in contrast, warm color shift
- **Particle size:** Fine mist, not individually visible
- **Density:** Very low

**Rain Effects:**
- **Wet surfaces:** Increased reflectivity (roughness reduced by 50%)
- **Water films:** Thin water layer on tiles (0.1-0.5mm)
- **Color shift:** Darker, more saturated colors when wet
- **Specular increase:** Glossy tiles become mirror-like
- **Drips and rivulets:** Water flowing down shells in channels

## VI. CONSTRUCTION DETAILS AND IMPERFECTIONS

### A. Tile Installation Variations

**Alignment Imperfections:**
- **Tile placement variation:** ±2mm from perfect grid
- **Frequency:** 5-10% of tiles slightly misaligned
- **Grout line variation:** 2-4mm width (nominal 3mm)
- **Pattern disruption:** Occasional chevron pattern misalignment at lid boundaries

**Tile Damage:**
- **Chipped tiles:** 0.1% of total (approximately 1,000 tiles)
- **Chip size:** 1-5mm edge chips
- **Chip location:** Corners and edges (impact damage)
- **Cracked tiles:** 0.05% (approximately 500 tiles)
- **Crack type:** Hairline cracks, star cracks from impact
- **Crack width:** 0.1-0.5mm

**Missing Tiles:**
- **Frequency:** Extremely rare, 1 in 10,000 tiles
- **Appearance:** Dark void showing concrete lid beneath
- **Location:** Random, typically where damage occurred
- **Temporary repair:** Sometimes filled with sealant (gray)

**Replacement Tiles:**
- **Replacement rate:** 0.5% over building lifetime
- **Visual difference:** Slightly brighter, cleaner than surrounding tiles
- **Color match:** Good but not perfect (ΔE = 3-5)
- **Gloss match:** Often slightly glossier (newer glaze)
- **Surrounding grout:** Cleaner, whiter around new tiles

### B. Concrete Imperfections

**Surface Defects:**
- **Air voids (blowholes):** 0.5-1% of surface area
- **Void size:** 5-20mm diameter
- **Void depth:** 5-15mm
- **Distribution:** Random, more common near formwork edges
- **Appearance:** Circular or irregular dark spots

**Pour Lines and Cold Joints:**
- **Visibility:** Subtle horizontal lines every 600mm (segment height)
- **Color variation:** ±5% luminosity across joint
- **Texture change:** Slight step or ridge (0.5-1mm)
- **Frequency:** Every segment (60-70 per rib)

**Form Tie Holes:**
- **Diameter:** 25mm
- **Spacing:** 600mm horizontally and vertically (grid pattern)
- **Patch quality:** Variable, some patches lighter or darker
- **Patch texture:** Smoother than surrounding concrete
- **Patch protrusion:** 0-2mm (some slightly proud)

**Aggregate Exposure:**
- **Frequency:** Occasional, <1% of surface
- **Appearance:** Rough patches where cement paste eroded
- **Aggregate size:** 10-20mm stones visible
- **Color:** Darker gray-brown (basalt aggregate)

**Cracking:**
- **Hairline cracks:** 1-2 per segment
- **Crack width:** 0.5-2mm
- **Crack pattern:** Random, some follow formwork lines
- **Crack depth:** Surface only (not structural)
- **Crack color:** Darker due to dirt accumulation (RGB 100, 100, 95)

**Spalling:**
- **Frequency:** 0.5% of surface area (approximately 130m² total)
- **Patch size:** 20-100mm diameter
- **Depth:** 5-20mm
- **Cause:** Rebar corrosion (rust expansion), freeze-thaw, impact
- **Appearance:** Irregular craters, exposed aggregate and rebar
- **Rust staining:** Orange-brown stains around spalled areas (RGB 180, 120, 80)
- **Repair evidence:** Some areas patched with lighter concrete

### C. Glass Installation Imperfections

**Panel Alignment:**
- **Variation from perfect:** ±3mm positional tolerance
- **Effect:** Slight irregularity in mullion grid
- **Visibility:** Noticeable in raking light, not obvious straight-on

**Sealant Quality:**
- **Bead consistency:** Variable, some beads thicker/thinner
- **Color variation:** Aging causes discoloration (black to gray-brown)
- **Cracking:** 10-20% of joints have fine cracks
- **Adhesion loss:** 5% of joints have slight gaps (<1mm)
- **Overflow:** Occasional sealant squeeze-out (1-2mm) on some panels

**Glass Defects:**
- **Scratches:** 1-3 per panel, 20-100mm long, 0.1-0.5mm wide
- **Impact marks:** 0.5% of panels have small chips (1-5mm)
- **Stress cracks:** Rare, radiating from impact points
- **Optical distortion:** Minimal, <0.5mm/m (high quality glass)
- **Tint variation:** Slight color shift between panels (±2% ΔE)

### D. Structural Movement and Settlement

**Expansion Joints:**
- **Joint width:** 20-30mm (varies with temperature)
- **Joint fill:** Flexible sealant, often degraded or missing
- **Joint color:** Dark gray to black (original), gray-brown (aged)
- **Joint location:** Every 12-15 meters in podium, at shell bases

**Settlement Evidence:**
- **Hairline cracks:** Following expansion joints
- **Crack width:** 1-3mm
- **Sealant failure:** Gaps or tears in joint sealant
- **Differential movement:** Slight steps at some joints (0.5-2mm)

**Thermal Movement:**
- **Daily cycle:** Shells expand/contract 5-10mm
- **Seasonal cycle:** 20-30mm total movement
- **Evidence:** Slightly wider joints in summer, narrower in winter
- **Wear patterns:** Polished edges on expansion joint faces

### E. Maintenance and Repair Evidence

**Cleaning Patterns:**
- **Recently cleaned areas:** Noticeably brighter tiles
- **Cleaning frequency:** Every 2-5 years (varies by area)
- **Cleaning method evidence:** Slight streaking, water marks
- **Uncleaned areas:** Gradual soiling accumulation

**Access Equipment Marks:**
- **Scratch marks:** On tiles and concrete from scaffolding, lifts
- **Scratch length:** 50-500mm
- **Scratch width:** 1-5mm
- **Scratch color:** Dark gray (metal marks) or lighter (surface removal)
- **Frequency:** 0.1% of surface area

**Repair Patches:**
- **Concrete repairs:** Lighter color, smoother texture
- **Patch size:** 50-500mm diameter
- **Frequency:** 1-2% of concrete surface
- **Tile repairs:** Replacement tiles (cleaner, brighter)
- **Sealant repairs:** Newer sealant (darker black) adjacent to aged sealant

**Graffiti Removal:**
- **Ghost images:** Faint staining where graffiti was removed
- **Color:** Slightly darker or discolored area
- **Size:** Variable, 100mm-1m patches
- **Frequency:** Rare, <0.1% of accessible surfaces
- **Location:** Lower podium areas, accessible walls

## VII. BLENDER MODELING STRATEGY

### A. Modeling Approach by Detail Level

**Level of Detail 1: Distant Views (>200m)**
- **Shell geometry:** Smooth subdivided surfaces, no individual ribs
- **Tile pattern:** Single texture with normal map
- **Glass:** Simple transparent shader
- **Podium:** Simplified geometry, texture-based details
- **Polygon count:** 50,000-100,000 total
- **Texture resolution:** 1K-2K

**Level of Detail 2: Medium Views (50-200m)**
- **Shell geometry:** Ribs modeled as simple extrusions
- **Tile pattern:** Texture with displacement/bump
- **Glass:** Mullion grid modeled, glass as planes
- **Podium:** Basic geometric details (stairs, columns)
- **Polygon count:** 500,000-1,000,000 total
- **Texture resolution:** 2K-4K

**Level of Detail 3: Close Views (10-50m)**
- **Shell geometry:** Detailed ribs with segments visible
- **Tile pattern:** Tile lids modeled, tiles as texture with displacement
- **Glass:** Full mullion detail, glass panels as separate objects
- **Podium:** Detailed stairs, columns, paving
- **Polygon count:** 2,000,000-5,000,000 total
- **Texture resolution:** 4K-8K

**Level of Detail 4: Hero Detail (<10m)**
- **Shell geometry:** Full rib detail with bolts and connections
- **Tile pattern:** Individual tiles modeled or instanced
- **Glass:** Full detail including gaskets and hardware
- **Podium:** Individual paving slabs, full architectural detail
- **Polygon count:** 10,000,000+ (use instancing to manage)
- **Texture resolution:** 8K-16K

### B. Geometry Creation Methods

**Shell Creation:**
1. **Create spherical sections:**
   - Create UV sphere, radius 75.2m
   - Delete unnecessary geometry, keep only shell section
   - Duplicate and position for each shell
   - Scale non-uniformly if needed (though ideally maintain spherical geometry)

2. **Rib structure:**
   - Create single rib profile curve (T-section, varies from 1.8m to 0.6m depth)
   - Array along shell surface (2.4m spacing)
   - Convert curves to mesh
   - Add segment divisions every 1.2-1.5m (cut lines)
   - Bevel edges for detail

3. **Alternative method (procedural):**
   - Use Geometry Nodes to generate ribs from shell surface
   - Curve-based rib generation with variable depth
   - Automatic segment creation
   - Instanced bolt objects at segment joints

**Tile System:**
1. **Tile lid method (medium detail):**
   - Create 2.4m × 1.2m panels
   - UV map for chevron tile texture
   - Displacement modifier for tile relief (0.5mm grout depth)
   - Array across shell surface
   - Snap to rib geometry

2. **Individual tiles (hero detail):**
   - Create single tile (120mm × 120mm)
   - Instance across lid surface using particle system or geometry nodes
   - Two tile types: glossy and matte (separate materials)
   - Chevron pattern using texture mask or manual placement
   - Slight random rotation/position variation (±1mm)

3. **Texture-only method (distant views):**
   - Apply chevron pattern texture directly to smooth shell
   - Normal map for grout lines
   - Bump map for subtle surface variation

**Glass Curtain Wall:**
1. **Mullion grid:**
   - Create single vertical mullion (150mm × 75mm profile)
   - Array horizontally (1.5m spacing)
   - Create horizontal mullions (same profile)
   - Array vertically (2.0m spacing)
   - Boolean union or join into single object

2. **Glass panels:**
   - Create planes (1.5m × 2.0m)
   - Inset 40mm from mullion face
   - Solidify modifier (13.5mm thickness)
   - Separate objects for each panel (allows individual control)

3. **Curved sections:**
   - Use Bezier curves to define curved glass wall paths
   - Array/follow path for mullions
   - Shrinkwrap glass panels to curved surface

**Podium:**
1. **Base platform:**
   - Create box (185m × 120m × 25m)
   - Add staircase geometry (97 steps, 400mm × 150mm)
   - Model landings as separate planes
   - Handrails as curve objects

2. **Granite paving:**
   - Array of planes (1.2m × 0.6m)
   - Slight random height variation (±1mm)
   - Grout lines via UV mapping or geometry

3. **Columns:**
   - Single column (800mm × 800mm × 8m)
   - Array (6m spacing)
   - Chamfer top edges (50mm)

### G. Optimization Techniques

**Instancing:**
- Use instances for repeated elements (tiles, columns, mullions)
- Reduces memory usage dramatically
- Enable "Instance Collection" for tile arrays

**Level of Detail (LOD):**
- Create multiple versions of model at different detail levels
- Use distance-based switching (manual or via add-ons)
- Hero model for close-ups, simplified for distant views

**Texture Optimization:**
- Use texture atlases for small repeated elements
- Compress textures (PNG or JPEG) for non-hero assets
- Use procedural textures where possible (reduces memory)

**Geometry Optimization:**
- Use subdivision surface modifiers instead of dense base geometry
- Enable "Optimal Display" for subdivision surfaces
- Use displacement only on hero assets, normal maps for distant views

**Render Optimization:**
- Use render layers to separate foreground (detailed) from background (simplified)
- Render shells and podium separately, composite in post
- Use holdout materials to mask areas not in frame
- Enable "Persistent Images" in Cycles (speeds up animation)

**Memory Management:**
- Monitor memory usage in System Console
- Reduce texture resolution if exceeding RAM
- Use external file references for large textures (link instead of pack)
- Close other applications during rendering

## IX. FINAL NOTES AND RECOMMENDATIONS

### Modeling Priorities
1. **Start with overall form:** Block out shells, podium, and site
2. **Add primary structure:** Rib system, glass walls, stairs
3. **Refine materials:** Develop shader networks for key materials
4. **Add details progressively:** Tiles, hardware, weathering based on camera distance
5. **Optimize continuously:** Use instancing, LODs, and efficient geometry

### Common Pitfalls to Avoid
- **Over-modeling distant elements:** Use textures and normal maps instead
- **Ignoring scale:** Sydney Opera House is massive; ensure correct proportions
- **Perfect geometry:** Add subtle imperfections for realism (misalignment, weathering)
- **Uniform materials:** Add color variation, dirt, and wear
- **Flat lighting:** Use atmospheric effects and complex light interactions

### Realism Checklist
- [ ] Correct overall proportions and dimensions
- [ ] Spherical shell geometry (75.2m radius)
- [ ] Chevron tile pattern oriented toward apex
- [ ] Glossy/matte tile distribution (70%/30%)
- [ ] Rib spacing (2.4m) consistent
- [ ] Glass bronze tint and reflectivity
- [ ] Granite pink-beige color and flamed texture
- [ ] Bronze patina (green-brown) on all fixtures
- [ ] Weathering: salt spray, water stains, biological growth
- [ ] Imperfections: tile chips, concrete spalling, misalignments
- [ ] Atmospheric effects: haze, reflections, caustics
- [ ] Proper lighting: sun intensity, sky color, ambient occlusion
- [ ] Context: harbor water, surrounding cityscape

### Artistic Considerations
- **Composition:** Use rule of thirds, leading lines (stairs, ribs)
- **Lighting:** Golden hour provides most dramatic and beautiful light
- **Weather:** Overcast softens shadows, rain adds reflections and drama
- **Time of day:** Sunset/sunrise for warm tones, midday for stark contrast, night for dramatic uplighting
- **Camera angle:** Low angles emphasize monumentality, aerial shows geometric arrangement
- **Depth of field:** Use sparingly; architectural photography typically has deep focus

### Performance Benchmarks
- **Viewport performance:** Aim for 15-30 FPS with simplified geometry
- **Render time:** 2-10 minutes per frame at 1080p (depending on samples and complexity)
- **Memory usage:** 8-16 GB RAM for full detail model
- **File size:** 500 MB - 2 GB for complete scene with textures
